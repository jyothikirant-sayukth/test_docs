# Field Inspector
The Field Inspector is responsible for validating a random number of taxable properties within the Panchayat to ensure the accuracy of the survey. Additionally, the Field Inspecctor plays a key role in managing corrections and assisting in the absence of the Panchayat Secretary.
{{< notice "note" >}} The Support Team will create the Field Inspector's account.{{< /notice >}}

## Persona
1. <b>Qualification:</b> A Graduate residing in the native place.
1. Should create a supportive environment with the Panchayat Secretary to make operations smoother.
1. Should have basic knowledge of device and incident management tools.
1. Should have strong regional communication skills with professional attire.
1. Must communicate well with the Panchayat Secretary and local people.

## Responsibilities
1. Responsible for managing the property inspection process, including generating random property selections, downloading property data for verification, and updating inspection data with approval/rejection statuses.
1. Responsible for collecting Panchayat devices and printed survey data, then handing them over to the Panchayat Secretary.
1. Responsible for updating the Device Incident Status to "Device Delivered."
1. <b>Correction of Surveyed Data:</b> Responsible for updating surveyed data if the citizen requests changes.
1. In the absence of the Panchayat Secretary:
   - Perform all operations except financial transactions.
   - Act as the primary person for making corrections to the surveyed data.

### Property Inspection Process  
The Field Inspector validates random properties from the surveyed data to ensure the survey's accuracy.

#### Navigation
1. Look for the <b>Property Inspection</b> option in the side menu on the left side of the screen.
1. Click on the <b>Inspect Data</b> category will be displayed within the Property Inspection section.
1. The <b>Generate Properties</b> option.
1. Click on the <b>Generate Properties</b> category.
1. You will be taken to the Property Generation Screen.

### GIS Maps 
1. Look for the <b>GIS Maps</b> option in the side menu on the left side of the screen, and then find the <b>Taxable Properties</b> within the GIS Maps; clicking on it will take you to the Property Geo Map screen.
   1. You can view the location of taxable properties, Select the checkbox next to a property name to view its location on the map.
1. Look for the <b>Panchayat Asset</b> within the GIS Maps; clicking on it will take you to the Asset Geo Map screen.
   1. You can view the location of Panchayat Asset, Select the checkbox next to a property name to view its location on the map.
1. Look for the <b>Panchayat Device</b> option in the side menu on the left side of the screen, and then find the <b>Device Incident</b> within the Panchayat Device; clicking on it will take you to the Device Incident Screen.

#### Generate Random Properties
{{< notice "note" >}} Survey data for inspection can only be generated if the Panchayat survey is fully completed and the survey status is in the "Survey Completed" state. {{< /notice >}}
On the Property Generation Screen, click on the <b>Generate</b> button to create random properties for inspection.<br>
{{< notice "note">}}
The generated properties will be based on the total properties surveyed within the Panchayat.{{< /notice >}}<br>
1. Select the Panchayat for which random properties need to be generated.
1. Enter the number of properties to generate and click on the <b>Generate</b> button.
1. The random properties will be displayed on the screen for validation.

##### Verify Properties

1. Review each property displayed for verification purposes.  
1. Cross-check the property details of the printed survey data.
1. Update the Inspection Status as Approved or Rejected for each property.  
1. At the bottom right corner, you can find the <b>Back</b> button, which navigates back to the Property Inspection Screen.  
1. If everything is fine after verifying the properties, submit the data and ensure the device is in working condition before handing it to the Panchayat Secretary. 
1. Once all required fields are updated, click the <b>Submit</b> button.  
1. A message will be displayed at the top of the screen as <b>Properties Successfully Verified</b>.
