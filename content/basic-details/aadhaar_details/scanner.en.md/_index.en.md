### Aadhaar QR/OCR

{{< notice "note">}}
If the Aadhaar card is a PVC card, you must capture both the front and back sides; however, if it is the original paper Aadhaar letter, capturing only the front side is enough.{{< /notice >}} <br>

The following fields will be captured by scanning the Aadhaar QR/OCR :
- Aadhaar Number
- Name
- Surname
- Father/Spouse Name
- Gender
- Date of Birth
- Age
- Address
1. You should scan the Aadhaar QR from a distance of <b>20 cm</b> from the mobile camera within <b>15 seconds</b>.  
1. If you do not scan the QR within this time, a message will be displayed at the bottom of the screen <b>"QR not detected, switching to OCR"</b>.
1. In OCR(Optical Character Recognition), you can take a photo of the Aadhaar card from <b>1 foot</b> away and make sure the camera focus on the Aadhaar Card.
1. Focus on the Aadhaar front side, click on the <b>Capture</b> button then a message will displays as <b> "Front Side Captured"</b>.
1. Now, Focus on the back side and click on the <b>Capture</b> button again then a message will displays as <b>"Back Side Captured"</b> and it will moves to the Create screen.
